Why Kitengela is a Top Weekend Destination in 2026

For a long time, many people viewed Kitengela simply as a busy residential town on the outskirts of Nairobi. Today, it has evolved into one of the most exciting choices for a quick, action‑packed weekend getaway. The town offers a brilliant mix of artistic wonders, high‑adrenaline outdoor parks, and a world‑famous culinary scene. It provides an ideal break from city life without requiring days of travel planning. Just 30 minutes from Nairobi via the Expressway, Kitengela is accessible enough for a day trip but charming enough to warrant an overnight stay.

Whether you’re travelling solo, as a couple, or with family, this guide covers the best activities, accommodation options, and a complete itinerary to make your weekend unforgettable.

Top 7 Things to Do in Kitengela on a Weekend

1. Explore the Whimsical Kitengela Hot Glass Studio

📍 Location: Oloosirkon, near Nairobi National Park
💰 Cost: Free entry; glassblowing workshop KSh 1,500‑3,000
🕐 Hours: Tue‑Sat 8am‑4:30pm
Tucked away on the edge of Nairobi National Park is Anselm’s famous glass studio. This architectural wonderland lets you watch master artisans melt recycled bottles and window glass into stunning, colourful sculptures. Walk across the famous, adrenaline‑pumping suspension bridge dangling over a deep river gorge, or sign up for a hands‑on glassblowing experience to create your own souvenir. Pro‑Tip: The live studio glassblowing demonstrations are completely free! Combine this with a picnic – there’s a beautiful cliffside café.

2. Conquer the Obstacle Courses at Nkasiri Adventure Park

📍 Location: Off Namanga Road, ~45km from Nairobi
💰 Cost: KSh 1,500‑2,500 per activity
🕐 Hours: 9am‑6pm daily
If you are looking for an active, high‑energy afternoon, head to Nkasiri Adventure Park. Test your fitness on the multi‑tiered high ropes courses, fly through the air on the zipline, or try archery and paintballing. The park also features a refreshing outdoor swimming pool to cool off afterward. It’s perfect for groups of friends, couples, and active families.

3. Indulge in World‑Famous Kitengela Nyama Choma

📍 Best Spots: Acacia Junction, Choma Zone, Milimani Roadside Grills
💰 Budget: KSh 500‑1,200 per person
No trip to Kitengela is complete without sampling its signature delicacy. Kitengela is legendary across Kenya for serving some of the juiciest, most expertly grilled nyama choma (roasted meat). Popular open‑air joints along Namanga Road serve fresh, tender goat meat paired with hot ugali and kachumbari. For a modern twist, try Choma Zone – a sports bar with cocktails, live DJ on Fridays, and excellent chicken wings.

4. Family Fun at Kids' Amusement Parks

If you’re traveling with children, Kitengela features excellent dedicated family fun centres. Hotspots like GMC Place Kitengela and Rayan Park offer massive children’s amusement parks filled with water slides, swimming pools, bouncy castles, and family‑friendly restaurants. Entry fees range from KSh 300‑800 per child.

5. Safari Walk & Nature Trails (Nearby National Park)

For nature lovers, Kitengela’s proximity to Nairobi National Park allows for morning game drives or a visit to the Safari Walk where you can spot lions, rhinos, and giraffes just minutes from your Airbnb. A game drive (self‑drive) costs KSh 1,500 for citizens (KSh 4,300 for non‑residents).

6. Glass Sculpture Workshop (Hands‑On)

At Kitengela Hot Glass, you can book a 2‑hour workshop where you’ll make your own glass pendant or paperweight. It’s a unique, memorable souvenir. Cost: ~KSh 2,500 per person. Advance booking recommended.

7. Evening Drinks & Live Music

End your day at Club Silk (largest nightclub) or The Loft Lounge (rooftop bar with shisha). For live bands, Stopover Bar & Restaurant has music on Saturdays. Many nyama choma joints also transform into lively bars after 9pm.

Sample 2‑Day Kitengela Weekend Itinerary

  • Saturday Morning (9am): Leave Nairobi via Expressway (30‑40 min). Check into your Airbnb (many offer early luggage drop-off).
  • Saturday Late Morning (10am‑1pm): Visit Kitengela Hot Glass – watch glassblowing, walk the suspension bridge, have lunch at the cliffside café.
  • Saturday Afternoon (2pm‑5pm): Head to Nkasiri Adventure Park for ziplining, ropes course, and archery.
  • Saturday Evening (6pm‑9pm): Nyama choma feast at Acacia Junction or Choma Zone. Try local Tuskers or a cocktail.
  • Saturday Night (9pm‑late): If you still have energy, club at Club Silk or relax at The Loft Lounge.
  • Sunday Morning (8am‑11am): Sleep in, cook a relaxed breakfast in your Airbnb kitchen.
  • Sunday Late Morning (11am‑1pm): Family fun at GMC Place or Rayan Park (if with kids). Alternatively, a nature walk near the Athi Plains.
  • Sunday Afternoon (1pm‑3pm): Quick lunch, check out, and drive back to Nairobi (30‑40 min).

This itinerary works for couples, families, and solo travellers – just adjust activity intensity.

Cost Breakdown – Budget vs Mid‑Range Weekend (2 days, 1 night)

  • Budget (solo or couple): Airbnb studio (KSh 2,500/night) + meals (KSh 1,500) + activities (Hot Glass free, Nkasiri KSh 1,500) + transport (fuel KSh 1,000) = ≈ KSh 6,500 per person.
  • Mid‑range (family of 4): 2‑bed Airbnb (KSh 5,000/night) + meals (KSh 4,000) + activities (Hot Glass workshop KSh 2,500, Nkasiri KSh 2,000 per person) + transport (KSh 1,500) = ≈ KSh 15,000 total.

Compared to a weekend in Naivasha or Amboseli, Kitengela offers similar fun at half the cost.

Packing Tips for a Kitengela Weekend

  • Comfortable walking shoes (for Hot Glass bridge and Nkasiri).
  • Swimsuit and towel (pool at Nkasiri and some Airbnbs).
  • Sunscreen and hat – Kitengela can be hot (28‑32°C).
  • Insect repellent (even though mosquitoes are few, rainy season has some).
  • Power bank – if you’re taking many photos/videos.
  • Cash – some roadside nyama choma joints don’t accept M‑Pesa.

Frequently Asked Questions (Weekend Guide)

How far is Kitengela from Nairobi?

Approximately 30 kilometres via Mombasa Road. Using the Expressway, you can be there in 30‑45 minutes outside peak traffic hours.

What is the best time to visit Kitengela for a weekend getaway?

The weather is warm year‑round, but the dry seasons (January‑February and June‑October) are ideal for outdoor activities.

Can I do a day trip from Nairobi to Kitengela?

Absolutely. However, staying overnight allows you to fully relax and experience the evening nyama choma scene without rushing back.

What's the best nyama choma spot in Kitengela?

Acacia Junction and Choma Zone are the most famous. Acacia Junction offers a traditional roadside experience; Choma Zone has a modern sports bar vibe with cocktails.
