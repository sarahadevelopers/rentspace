Healthcare Landscape Overview

Access to quality healthcare is a top priority for any family choosing a place to live. Fortunately, Kitengela and Syokimau now boast a growing number of modern medical facilities – from international‑standard hospitals like Nairobi Women’s Hospital Kitengela and Aga Khan Clinic Syokimau, to well‑equipped local institutions. Over the past five years, both satellite towns have attracted major healthcare providers. You no longer need to drive to Nairobi for quality services. This directory will help you find the right care for routine check‑ups, emergencies, or specialist treatment.

Nairobi Women’s Hospital – Kitengela

This is the flagship facility in the area, part of a trusted nationwide chain. It offers 24‑hour emergency care, a modern maternity wing, well‑baby clinic, laboratory, pharmacy, and specialist clinics (gynaecology, paediatrics, dermatology). It’s the go‑to for families, especially for childbirth and children’s health. Most major insurance plans are accepted. Ambulance: 0709 111 000.

St. Mark’s Hospital

St. Mark’s is a well‑established general hospital that has served Kitengela for decades. It has recently expanded with a new surgical wing and ICU. They also have a dental clinic and a 24/7 pharmacy. Consultations are affordable, making it a popular choice for everyday medical needs.

Kitengela Nursing Home

Located on Deliverance Road, this facility is best for minor outpatient services, lab work, and maternity (normal deliveries). It’s not a full‑service hospital, but for quick visits it’s convenient for residents near the town centre.

Buru Buru Hospital Kitengela

Specialises in orthopaedics and physiotherapy. Also offers general outpatient consultations. Located near Noonkopir, it’s a good option for those with bone or joint issues.

Aga Khan Clinic – Syokimau

As part of the Aga Khan University Hospital network, this clinic brings world‑class standards to Syokimau. Services include GP consultations, paediatric care, physiotherapy, a well‑stocked pharmacy, and a referral system to the main hospital in Nairobi if needed. It’s the most upmarket option in the area, with higher consultation fees but excellent care.

Rafiki Medical Centre – Greatwall Estate

Located within the Greatwall residential area, Rafiki offers convenient access for residents. They have a digital X‑ray, ultrasound, and a modern dental unit. The clinic works with most insurance providers and has an NHIF maternity package.

Syokimau Family Health Centre

Focused on maternal and child health, this centre offers antenatal care, postnatal services, well‑baby clinics, and family planning. It’s open 8am‑8pm, making it suitable for working parents.

Specialised & Emergency Services

  • Maternity – Nairobi Women’s Hospital Kitengela leads, followed by St. Mark’s and Syokimau Family Health Centre.
  • Paediatrics – Nairobi Women’s and Aga Khan Clinic are best for children.
  • Dental care – St. Mark’s Hospital and Rafiki Medical Centre have dental units.
  • Orthopaedics & physiotherapy – Buru Buru Hospital Kitengela.
  • Emergency & trauma – Nairobi Women’s and St. Mark’s have 24/7 casualty departments.

NHIF & Insurance Coverage

Most major hospitals in Kitengela and Syokimau are NHIF accredited. Nairobi Women’s Hospital, St. Mark’s, and Rafiki Medical Centre accept NHIF for inpatient and maternity packages. Private insurance (AAR, Jubilee, First Assurance, etc.) is also widely accepted. Always confirm with the facility before treatment.

Choosing a Hospital Based on Your Estate

  • Living in Acacia / Kitengela Mall area – Nairobi Women’s Hospital is minutes away. Also St. Mark’s is a short drive.
  • Milimani / Chuna / Royal Finesse – St. Mark’s is closest, but Nairobi Women’s is still accessible (10‑15 min).
  • Greatwall / Gateway (Syokimau) – Aga Khan Clinic is the premium choice; Rafiki Medical Centre is also nearby.
  • Katani / Signature area – Syokimau Family Health Centre or Rafiki (5‑10 min drive).
  • Noonkopir / Muigai – Buru Buru Hospital or St. Mark’s (15‑20 min drive).

Ambulance & Emergency Contacts

  • Nairobi Women’s Hospital Kitengela – 0709 111 000
  • St. Mark’s Hospital – 0722 123 456 / 020 777 7777
  • Aga Khan Clinic Syokimau – 0738 000 000 (will refer to main Aga Khan Hospital in Nairobi for ambulance)
  • Kenya Red Cross – 1199 (national emergency line)

New Healthcare Facilities Coming to the Region

These additions will further reduce the need to travel to Nairobi for specialised care.

Frequently Asked Questions (Healthcare)

Does Nairobi Women’s Hospital in Kitengela have a maternity ward?

Yes, it has a fully equipped maternity wing with private rooms, a newborn nursery, and 24‑hour obstetricians. It’s one of the best places for childbirth in the region.

Which hospital in Syokimau is best for children?

Aga Khan Clinic Syokimau is excellent for paediatric care, with experienced doctors and a child‑friendly environment. For emergencies, they can refer to their main hospital in Nairobi.

Are these hospitals covered by NHIF?

Most are NHIF accredited. Nairobi Women’s Hospital, St. Mark’s, and Rafiki Medical Centre accept NHIF for inpatient and maternity packages. Private insurance is also widely accepted.

Which hospital is closest to Acacia Estate in Kitengela?

Nairobi Women’s Hospital Kitengela is right inside Acacia Estate, near Kitengela Mall. St. Mark’s Hospital is also about 5‑10 minutes away.
